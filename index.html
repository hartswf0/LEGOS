Understanding LEGOS

LEGOS (Location, Entity, Goal, Obstacle, Shift, Solution) is a framework for narrative generation. Each element plays a specific role in creating compelling stories.

Location (L)

The setting or environment where your story takes place.

Example: "An Isolated Cabin" — A remote wilderness retreat cut off from the outside world by severe winter storms.

Entity (E)

The characters or beings that drive your narrative.

Example: "A Hopeless Introvert" — A lonely programmer who has gone months without meaningful human contact.

Goal (G)

What Entities desire, need, or seek to achieve.

Example: "Making a Human Connection" — Finding a way to overcome social anxiety to form a genuine relationship.

Obstacle (O)

Challenges or conflicts that prevent Entities from achieving their Goals.

Example: "A Mysterious ATM" — A strange device that only displays deeply personal questions instead of dispensing cash.

Shift (S)

Turning points or revelations that alter the narrative's trajectory.

Example: "Voice to Water" — A moment when spoken words begin to materialize as flowing liquid, making communication visible but ephemeral.

Solution (U)

Resolutions to conflicts that emerge when Entities overcome Obstacles to achieve Goals.

Example: "Echo Marriage" — Finding connection not with another person but with the resonance of one's own voice returned through unexpected channels.

A Complete Narrative Example:

In An Isolated Cabin (L), A Hopeless Introvert (E) is determined to overcome their isolation by Making a Human Connection (G). Their attempts are complicated by A Mysterious ATM (O) that appears in their living room, offering profound insights but no practical help. Everything changes when they experience Voice to Water (S) — every word they speak materializes as luminous droplets, making their thoughts tangible. This leads to Echo Marriage (U), as they learn to find connection not with others but with the externalized aspects of their own consciousness.
